What is a Golden Ratio?

A golden ratio is a number of a line divided into two segments. The ratio of the whole is the same to the larger segment as the larger segment is to the shorter segment. A golden ratio is an irrational number, which means a number that never ends. Mathematically the golden ratio can be written as (1 + sqrt(5)) / 2 = 1.61803398875…


Other names for the golden ratios are the golden section, golden mean, divine proportion, mean ratio, proportionally divided, continuous proportion, goldene schnitt or a golden number.
